Interlace Forest

Digital Tectonics Forestation by Human Robot Collaboration

Robotic Fabrication Human Robot Collaboration Full Scale tectonics
Year 2022
Research Focus Digital Tectonics Forestation
Instructors Shen, Yang-Ting; Yen, Chia-Ching
Team Kuo, Chien-Kai    Zheng, Chong-Ming
Fu, Cheng-Wen    Lee, Yi-Xuan
Wong, Pak-Hei    Lee, Zhi-Yuan

This project emphasizes the concept of Technology-Empowered Design. Through this approach, we explore and practice the integration of digital real-structure design assisted by robotic arms. By connecting design, manufacturing, and construction, we aim to build a seamless and highly coordinated workflow.

The project is carried out in two stages. The first stage focuses on parametric thinking and design training, while the second stage transitions into digital fabrication, ultimately delivering a full-scale wooden pavilion.

Wood serves as the primary construction material. By applying drilling, milling, and cutting techniques, we turn the material’s inherent constraints into opportunities for design exploration and fabrication challenges, paving the way for practical and innovative applications.

The pavilion is organized as four hierarchical layers of branches. Primary trunks make up the first level and therefore carry the largest cross-section to support the structure.

Its roof is generated with a Voronoi diagram so that loads flow naturally toward the nearest columns. Because each member length is constrained by the fabrication setup, the roof frame is carefully optimized to satisfy both structural performance and manufacturing limits.

To enable robotic assembly, the tenon bottoms are trimmed slightly smaller than the tops, creating a tolerance that guides each member into position. Components are delivered in sequence so the structure locks itself while maintaining alignment.

Upper supports are cut along planar surfaces to connect with the roof, while corner tenons prevent lateral movement. The roof module combines a frame and interlocking joints, using five joint types capable of resolving multiple angles and complex geometries.
